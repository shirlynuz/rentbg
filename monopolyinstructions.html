Monopoly The Singapore Edition

Setting up

Remaining money goes to the bank

Banks: What does it do?

  1. Choose one player to be banker
  2. Banks hold money, Title deedes, houses and hotels prior to purchase by players
  3. Banks pay salaries and sells houses
  4. Sells houses and collects taxes, fines and interests

How to play

• Players rolls dice, player with highest number starts the play

• Place your token on "Go", throw the dice and move your token accordingly, in the direction of the arrow

❗ Throwing doubles three times in succession will land you in Jail ❗

☺ A player gets $200 when his/her token lands on or passes over "Go" ☺

• When you land on an unowned property, you can purchase that property from the Bank at its printed price

• However when you land on an owned property, the owner collects rent from you (amount is stated in the Title Deed card)

• Owner can charge double the rent if he owns all the properties in the same colour group

• Owning houses and hotels on that property will be an advantage because owner can collect a higher rent

• Hotels can only be bought after purchasing 3 houses

Chance

Take the top card from the deck when you land on "Chance" or "Community chest" and follow the instructions

The "Get out of jail" card is held until use or can be sold to players at a price agreeable to both

Jail

You go to Jail when you:

• Land on "Go to Jail"

• Draw a card marked "Go to Jail"

• Throw doubles three times in succession

Getting out of jail

• Throwing doubles on any of your next three turns, if you succeed in doing this you immediately move forward the number of spaces shown by your doubles throw.

• Using the "Get Out of Jail Free Card"

• Paying a fine of $50 before you roll the dice on either of your next two turns. If you do not throw doubles by your third turn, you must pay the $50 fine

Winning the game

Game ends when a second bankruptcy occurs

Bankrupt players to retire. Remaining players to value their property:

• Cash

• Lots, Utilities and Railroads owned, at the price printed on the board.

• Houses and hotels(to value with the 3 houses)

Richest player wins
